01

The story so far

Two years ago I walked into CAMS with an MBA and honestly no real clue what "PMO" meant day to day. Turns out it means this: whatever needs doing and nobody owns yet, becomes yours.

My first real project was cost optimization, cutting spend across 23 business units. We landed at around 3.7% in annual savings without dropping coverage anywhere. That's the project that got me into rooms I hadn't earned my way into yet.

From there it was business and revenue cadence. Fortnightly reviews across 22 lines, monthly ones across 8 verticals. Real ownership, but still work with a manual to follow.

About seven months in, things changed. I got asked to build a CFO Office that didn't exist yet. No predecessor, no template, nobody to ask. Just a deadline on top of everything else I was already running.

It worked out. Three months later it was running on its own, and I was back to full-time in the MD's Office like nothing had changed.

Same instinct since, just under different names. Find what's unstructured, give it a spine. I'm looking for the next room that needs exactly that.

3 months. Mandate to operating CFO Office — governance, cadence, sign-off, running.
